Welcome to Outer Space

“The solar system should be viewed as our backyard, not as some sequence of destinations that we do one at a time.”

- Neil Degrasse Tyson

Check Out Some Of These Cool Facts!

1.Space is completely silent because there is no air to carry sound waves.

2. One million Earths could fit inside the Sun, which is about 109 times wider than Earth.

3. A day on Venus is longer than a year—Venus takes 243 Earth days to rotate once but only 225 days to orbit the Sun.

4. Neutron stars are so dense that a sugar-cube-sized amount of material weighs more than a mountain.

5. Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a storm that has been raging for over 300 years and could fit Earth inside it.

6. Space isn't completely empty—it's full of "dark matter" and "dark energy" that we cannot see but make up most of the universe.

7. If you could drive a car to the Moon at 60 mph, it would take about 6 months to get there.

8. There are more stars in the universe than grains of sand on all the beaches on Earth.

9. The Sun's core reaches temperatures of about 27 million degrees Fahrenheit.

10. A day on Mars is only slightly longer than a day on Earth—about 24 hours and 39 minutes.

11. Black holes can "spaghettify" objects due to their immense gravitational pull, stretching them like noodles.

12. Saturn’s rings are made mostly of water ice and some rock, and they are about 282,000 km across but only 10 meters thick.

13. Light from the Sun takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth.

14. The largest mountain in the solar system is Olympus Mons on Mars, which is 3 times taller than Mount Everest.

15. The International Space Station (ISS) travels at 5 miles per second and orbits Earth about every 90 minutes.

16. Venus is the hottest planet in the solar system, even though Mercury is closer to the Sun, due to its thick atmosphere trapping heat.

17. A single teaspoon of a neutron star would weigh about 6 billion tons.

18. The footprints left by Apollo astronauts on the Moon will remain there for millions of years since there’s no wind to erode them.

19. Pluto is smaller than the United States—it’s about 1,400 miles across.

20. Stars twinkle because their light is distorted as it passes through Earth’s atmosphere.

21. The Milky Way galaxy is about 105,000 light-years wide and contains an estimated 100–400 billion stars.

22. You weigh less on the Moon—about 1/6th of your weight on Earth due to lower gravity.

23. The hottest star is about 200,000 times hotter than the Sun, reaching 200,000 Kelvin.

24. The "Kuiper Belt" is a region beyond Neptune containing many icy objects, including Pluto.

25. Traveling at the speed of light, it would still take 100,000 years to cross the Milky Way galaxy.

26. The Moon is drifting away from Earth at a rate of 1.5 inches per year.

27. A day on Mercury lasts 59 Earth days.

28. There is a planet made of diamond called "55 Cancri e," about 40 light-years away.

29. Astronauts can grow up to 2 inches taller in space due to the lack of gravity compressing their spines.

30. The largest asteroid in the solar system is Ceres, which is about 600 miles wide.

31. Earth is the densest planet in the solar system.

32. Uranus rotates on its side, with its axis tilted 98 degrees.

33. The Sun accounts for about 99.8% of the mass in the solar system.

34. A day on Jupiter lasts only 10 hours, making it the fastest spinning planet.

35. The Hubble Space Telescope has traveled over 3 billion miles since its launch in 1990.

36. It rains diamonds on Jupiter and Saturn due to extreme pressure turning carbon into diamond crystals.

37. Venus rotates clockwise, opposite to most other planets.

38. The coldest temperature ever recorded in space is -454 degrees Fahrenheit in the Boomerang Nebula.

39. Saturn’s moon Titan has rivers, lakes, and seas of liquid methane and ethane.

40. The Andromeda Galaxy is moving toward the Milky Way and will collide with it in about 4.5 billion years.

41. The closest star system to Earth is Alpha Centauri, located 4.37 light-years away.

42. Quasars are the brightest and most powerful objects in the universe, powered by supermassive black holes.

43. The largest known star, UY Scuti, has a radius 1,700 times larger than the Sun.

44. Mars has the largest canyon in the solar system, Valles Marineris, which is over 2,500 miles long.

45. A pulsar can spin as fast as 716 times per second.

46. The Moon has "moonquakes" caused by tidal forces and cooling of its core.

47. The first living creature to orbit Earth was Laika, a dog sent by the Soviet Union in 1957.

48. There are around 3,500 exoplanets (planets outside our solar system) confirmed to date.

49. Neptune has the fastest winds in the solar system, reaching over 1,200 miles per hour.

50. Astronauts lose muscle mass in space because of microgravity, requiring daily exercise.

51. A light-year is about 5.88 trillion miles.

52. The Milky Way has a supermassive black hole at its center called Sagittarius A*.

53. Space probes like Voyager 1 have left the solar system and entered interstellar space.

54. Some stars are older than the universe itself due to measurement uncertainties.

55. The Crab Nebula was created by a supernova explosion observed on Earth in 1054 AD.

56. The temperature on Mars can drop to -195 degrees Fahrenheit at night.

57. Saturn has at least 83 confirmed moons, the most in the solar system.

58. The observable universe is estimated to be 93 billion light-years across.

59. Space smells like a mix of diesel fumes, barbecue, and gunpowder, as reported by astronauts.

60. The first human-made object to leave Earth’s atmosphere was the V-2 rocket in 1944.

61. Stars are born in clouds of dust and gas called nebulae.

62. Astronauts on the ISS see about 16 sunrises and sunsets every day due to orbiting Earth every 90 minutes.

63. Mercury has almost no atmosphere, which is why it experiences extreme temperature changes.

64. A black hole's gravity is so strong that not even light can escape from it.

65. Earth’s magnetic field protects us from harmful solar radiation.

66. The Kuiper Belt is home to dwarf planets like Eris, Makemake, and Haumea.

67. The Moon has no atmosphere, which means the sky is always black.

68. There are rogue planets that float freely in space, not orbiting any star.

69. The surface of the Sun is about 10,000 degrees Fahrenheit, but its corona can reach millions of degrees.

70. The Milky Way is part of a galaxy cluster called the Local Group.

71. The first human to walk in space was Alexei Leonov in 1965.

72. Red dwarf stars can live for trillions of years because they burn their fuel very slowly.

73. A year on Uranus lasts 84 Earth years.

74. The James Webb Space Telescope is designed to look deeper into the universe than any telescope before it.

75. Some black holes are surrounded by glowing accretion disks of gas and dust spiraling inward.
